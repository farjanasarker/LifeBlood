# Blood Donation Process FAQ

## How do I donate blood?
1. Find a nearby blood donation camp, hospital, or blood bank, or coordinate with a donor/recipient through the LifeBlood platform.
2. Bring a valid photo ID.
3. Fill out a donor health questionnaire about your medical history and recent activities.
4. A health professional checks your hemoglobin level, blood pressure, pulse, and weight.
5. If eligible, the actual blood draw takes about 8-10 minutes and collects approximately 350-450 ml of blood.
6. After donation, you rest for 10-15 minutes and are given a light snack and fluids.
7. The entire visit, including registration and screening, usually takes 30-45 minutes.

## What happens during the donation itself?
- You sit or lie in a reclined chair.
- A sterile, single-use needle is inserted into a vein in your arm.
- Blood flows into a collection bag; the process is mostly painless aside from the initial needle prick.
- Staff monitor you throughout for any signs of dizziness or discomfort.

## What should I do before donating?
- Eat a healthy meal and stay well-hydrated (drink extra water) before donating.
- Get a good night's sleep before the donation day.
- Avoid fatty foods right before donating.
- Avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours before donation.
- Wear clothing with sleeves that can be rolled up easily.

## Is donating blood safe?
- Yes. All equipment used (needles, bags, tubing) is sterile and single-use, discarded after one donation.
- There is no risk of contracting any disease from donating blood.

## How often can I donate?
- Whole blood: every 90 days (3 months) for both men and women, up to 4 times a year.

## How do I use the LifeBlood platform to find or respond to donation requests?
- Use the "Find Donors" / Search feature to search for compatible donors by blood group and location (division, district, upazila).
- Logged-in users can post a blood request with their required blood group, location, and deadline; nearby compatible donors are notified.
- Donors can view incoming notifications and accept or decline a request.
- Users can message each other directly via the in-app Chat feature once a request connection is made, and can enable/disable chat from Chat Settings.
- Donors can log their donation history and track total donations and badges from their Dashboard.

## What are the donor badges?
- "Hero Donor" and "Life Saver" badges are awarded based on a donor's total number of recorded donations, visible on their profile and dashboard.
