# Blood Donation Eligibility Guidelines

## Basic eligibility criteria
- A donor must be between 18 and 60 years old.
- A donor must weigh at least 50 kg (110 lbs).
- Hemoglobin level must be at least 12.5 g/dL.
- A donor must be in general good health and feeling well on the day of donation.
- Blood pressure should be within normal range (systolic 100-180, diastolic 60-100).

## Donation interval
- Whole blood donors must wait at least 90 days (3 months) between donations.
- Donors should not donate more than 4 times in a year.

## Conditions that make someone temporarily ineligible (defer donation)
- Common cold, flu, fever, or any active infection: wait until fully recovered, at least 7 days symptom-free.
- Recent tattoo or piercing: wait 6 months from the date it was done (due to infection risk window).
- Pregnancy: not eligible during pregnancy; must wait at least 6 months after delivery.
- Breastfeeding: generally advised to wait until breastfeeding is complete, consult a doctor.
- Recent major surgery: wait at least 6 months after major surgery, or until full recovery confirmed by a doctor.
- Recent minor surgery or dental work: wait at least 1-7 days depending on procedure; consult a doctor if unsure.
- Low hemoglobin / anemia: not eligible until hemoglobin levels return to the acceptable range.
- Recent blood transfusion: wait at least 1 year.
- Travel to malaria-endemic areas: wait at least 3 months after returning.
- Alcohol consumption: avoid donating within 24 hours of drinking alcohol.
- Menstruation: most people can donate during their period if hemoglobin levels are normal and they feel well; if feeling weak or anemic, it is better to wait or consult a doctor.
- Recent vaccination: wait period depends on vaccine type, generally 14 days for live vaccines and 48 hours for inactivated vaccines; consult a doctor for the specific vaccine.

## Conditions that make someone permanently ineligible
- HIV/AIDS diagnosis.
- Hepatitis B or Hepatitis C infection.
- Confirmed Chagas disease.
- Certain chronic heart conditions, uncontrolled high blood pressure, or recent heart attack/stroke (consult a doctor).
- Active cancer or currently undergoing cancer treatment (consult a doctor; some cancer survivors may be eligible after a remission period, decided case by case by a doctor).
- Intravenous (IV) drug use history (non-prescribed).

## Conditions that require medical consultation before donating
- Diabetes: generally eligible if well-controlled with stable medication and no complications, but should consult a doctor first.
- Thyroid disorders: generally eligible if condition is stable and medicated, consult a doctor.
- Asthma: generally eligible if not having an active attack and feeling well, consult a doctor if uncertain.
- Epilepsy: eligibility depends on how well controlled the condition is and how recent the last seizure was; consult a doctor.
- Any chronic illness not explicitly listed here.
- Taking blood thinners or other medication affecting clotting.

## General rule for unclear cases
If a donor's specific medical condition, medication, or recent procedure is not explicitly covered above, they should consult a doctor before donating to ensure both their own safety and the safety of the blood recipient.
