# Post-Donation Care Guidelines

## Immediately after donation
- Rest for at least 10-15 minutes at the donation site before leaving.
- Have a light snack and drink fluids (juice, water) provided at the donation site.
- Keep the bandage on the needle puncture site for at least 4-6 hours.

## For the next 24 hours
- Drink extra fluids (at least 3-4 liters of water) over the next 24-48 hours to help replace lost blood volume.
- Avoid strenuous physical activity, heavy lifting, or intense exercise for at least 24 hours.
- Avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours.
- Keep eating iron-rich foods (leafy greens, red meat, beans, lentils) for the next few days to help replenish iron levels.
- If the puncture site bleeds again, apply firm pressure with a clean cloth and raise the arm above heart level for a few minutes.
- Avoid smoking for a few hours after donation.

## Signs of normal post-donation reaction
- Mild fatigue or light-headedness for a few hours.
- Slight bruising or tenderness at the needle site.

## When to seek medical attention
- Fainting or prolonged dizziness that does not improve with rest.
- Continued bleeding from the puncture site after applying pressure.
- Signs of infection at the puncture site (increasing redness, swelling, warmth, or pus).
- Severe or persistent nausea, chest pain, or difficulty breathing.

## Age-based considerations
- Teen donors (18-19 years old): may feel the effects of fluid and blood volume loss more noticeably; should rest a bit longer, eat a nutritious meal soon after, and avoid sports or PE class for the rest of the day.
- Adult donors (20-59 years old): standard recovery guidance applies; most people resume normal daily activity the same day but should still avoid heavy exercise for 24 hours.
- Senior donors (60+ years, where locally permitted): should monitor more closely for dizziness or low blood pressure symptoms, rise slowly from sitting or lying positions, and rest longer before resuming activity.

## Weight considerations
- Donors closer to the minimum weight threshold (around 50 kg) may experience light-headedness more easily and should prioritize fluid and food intake immediately after donating and avoid standing up quickly.

## Donors with health conditions
- Donors with diabetes should monitor blood sugar levels after donating, since fasting or skipping meals around donation time can cause fluctuations; eating a balanced meal soon after donation is recommended.
- Donors with a history of low blood pressure or fainting should sit or lie down at the first sign of dizziness and avoid standing up suddenly for the rest of the day.
- Any donor who feels unusually unwell after donating should not hesitate to seek medical attention rather than waiting it out.
